What is the Euclidean Algorithm?

The Euclidean Algorithm is an efficient method for computing the greatest common divisor (GCD) of two integers. The GCD is the largest positive integer that divides both numbers without a remainder.

The algorithm works by repeatedly applying the division algorithm and using the fact that: gcd(a, b) = gcd(b, a mod b) until a mod b = 0.
